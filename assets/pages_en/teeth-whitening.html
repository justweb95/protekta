Teeth whitening

Teeth whitening is a safe procedure performed under the control of a dentist. The procedure involves applying a special gel that is activated with an LED UV lamp. The next step after the gel application is polishing the teeth with a fluoride-based protective paste. The treatment typically lasts from one to one and a half hours. After the procedure, the patient receives instructions and advice to follow the coming days to achieve the best possible teeth whitening results.

At-home teeth whitening – Procedure

During the first visit to the dentist, dental impressions are taken to create whitening trays.

During the next visit, the patient receives the finished trays and a set of whitening gels for at- home use, along with all instructions for use and post-treatment advice.

Contraindications

  • Individuals under the age of 18
  • Pregnant and breastfeeding women
  • Allergies to any of the gel ingredients
  • Increased tooth sensitivity
  • Individuals undergoing orthodontic treatment.
